    01

    Out of sight.
    Never out of reach.

    No dock icon, no window, no clutter. KeyHolder sits beside your clock and opens with a click or a global shortcut — search, copy, gone.

    a

    Instant recall

    Open the popover, type two letters, hit return. Your key is on the clipboard before the window finishes fading in.

    b

    Smart mapping

    Platform names resolve to icons and themes automatically — OpenAI, GitHub, Stripe, AWS — no manual organizing.

    c

    Auto-lock

    Click away and the popover vanishes and locks. Nothing lingers on screen, nothing stays decrypted.

    d

    Featherweight

    Pure SwiftUI on Mac, WPF on Windows. Under 1 MB, negligible memory, zero Chromium.

    02

    Hardware-backed.
    Nothing in cleartext.

    Metadata and secrets never travel together. Names and labels live in a local file — the secrets themselves go straight into the operating system's hardware-backed vault and never touch the disk in cleartext.

    • 2.1

      System vaults only

      Secrets are written to macOS Keychain Services and Windows Credential Locker — encrypted by the OS, backed by hardware.

    • 2.2

      Biometric gate

      Touch ID or Windows Hello is required before any secret is decrypted. Physical presence, every time.

    • 2.3

      Strictly local

      No servers, no sync, no analytics, no network calls. Your keys never leave the machine.

    • 2.4

      Fully auditable

      Every line is open source under MIT. Read it, build it yourself, trust nothing on faith.
